Little Island

The epidemic in the United States has slowed down, and now a new punch-in landmark has appeared! The "Little Island", which is difficult to look away from from its exterior design, looks like it is composed and propped up by blossoming tulips. From a distance, it looks more like floating on the water, and it is bound to become the next popular tourist attraction.

Little Island, located in Hudson River Park, is the first new free pier park to open in New York after the epidemic. Written by British genius designer Thomas Heatherwick, it is another eye-catching landmark that surprises the world after The Vesse, known as the "New York Eiffel Tower".



Inspired by the original wood piles of the old pier No. 54, Heatherwick Studio joined hands with MNLA, a local landscape design team in New York, to design 132 tulip-shaped concrete blocks on 280 concrete ground piles, creating a "floating island". visual. Each flower has a different height, size and weight, supporting the soil, lawn and trees.



Little Island, the brainchild of billionaire media mogul Barry Diller and fashion designer Diane von Furstenberg, said: "I want the park to be a whimsical oasis, a place to relax and explore, and Programs and performances are entertaining, educational and stimulating.‚Äù

Planting more than 350 kinds of flowers, a total of more than 60,000 bouquets and more than 100 trees, including shrubs, evergreens, and autumn leaves, allow tourists visiting in different seasons to enjoy the charming and surprising natural scenery at every corner.



In the park, there is an amphitheater with a capacity of 687 seats, a central plaza for food and beverages, a stage and a lawn. In addition to walking and sunbathing, you can also enjoy the stunning views of New York City and the Hudson River. An art season will be held in June, inviting hundreds of local artists and performers to bring free programs to tourists of all ages, turning the park into an art space, open until 1 am every day.
